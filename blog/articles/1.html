How web changed for me, and for the world



I started web development at a very young age and creating a presentation site for myself has always been a very good activity. Before arriving at this site I very regularly created several versions of my site, always with a different interface. At first I was afraid of css, so I started by making simple sites with text and images. Today I'm back to that simplicity, but for other reasons. I've archived some of my site versions since 2023, which I'd like to show you.

My site that you should talk to

futureofthe.tech/history/v1


In 2023 (I was 14) I made a site. The goal was to completely rethink user interfaces with a completely different approach. On this site, you can talk to a script who will answer you in somewhat the same way as I would. It's not an AI, but a list of questions and answers. When you ask a question, the AI looks for the most similar question in its list and answers it as indicated with sometimes a bit of humore.

It's not really a good interface because the information isn't easy to find, and often my script didn't have an answer to the question.
But I still find the idea very interesting. The site's idea is not to display content directly to the visitor, but to let him ask the robot for what he want.
In a way, the concept was a bit visionary. With the development of language models, it's not uncommon to see conversational agents, with whom we can ask questions on the site.

These chatbots often use the openai API, with a first prompt containing the page content and other information about the site.
For now, these chatbots are just another feature of the original site. But who knows, maybe in the near future websites will no longer present content directly. We'll ask the chatbot questions to find the information we're looking for.

We won't be visiting sites, we'll be talking to them.

My site with no purpose

In 2023 I made the strangest version of my personal website. The site displays my logo with social links and every few seconds presents a new random sentence or joke about programming.
What's strange is that when the visitor scrolls, there's no new content. Instead, a tourbiollon spins in the background, a screaming audio track plays and the page expands to allow endless scrolling.

futureofthe.tech/history/v2


You might ask what the purpose of this strange idea is. I could answer that it's because it's an artistic project or because it's soo fun.
But no. It's not any of that. If I spent time creating this site, it's simply because I had nothing else to do. I'd just started doing web development and I was very impressed by the infinite creation possibilities. I didn't make a site for money, or for school, or even to share anything to the world.

For me, the purpose of a site wasn't to display content with an elaborate design and a specific objective.
For me a site was just to try, explore and learn.

What is the purpose of a WebSite ?

In January 1991 the first public web servers were turned on. On 6 August 1991, Berners-Lee published a short summary of the World Wide Web project. Let's take a look at the page.

info.cern.ch/hypertext/WWW/TheProject.html

The page is just black text on a white background with blue links. It's neither pretty, nor impressive, but was it the goal ? Was it the goal of Berners-Lee to create an impressive web page with a lot of animation and interactives elements ?



No, it was not. Firstly it wasn't possible because CSS didn't exist, but above all, that wasn't the purpose of this page. The one and only purpose of this page was to introduce the World Wide Web. And the mission has been successfully accomplished: the site is separated by multiple pages and is totally readable and understandable. On top of that, the site only weighs 2.17 KB.

Before I was born the purpose of a site was to share informations to other persons. But what's now ?



On October 24, 2000, Google released Google Ads. Google Ads is a digital advertising service. Companies can pay Businesses can pay Google to create ads, and web creators can integrate Google Ads into their websites to earn money. On that day, Google changed the face of the Internet for the eternity.



According to estimates, more than half of all websites have ads on them. It's a lot. And that's why the purpose of websites has radically evolved. The main goal is no longer to share content but to attract as many visitors as possible. The Internet is mainly driven by the attention economy and that's a problem.
Websites with ads don't try to make good content, they don't try to share ideas, all they want is your attention. There's a big difference between attractive content and interesting content. The information doesn't have to be true or relevant to get your attention. A site with 1,000 news articles with exagerated content will attract more of your attention and therefore be more profitable.
That's why you can't trust services whose source of income is advertising or the sale of personal data.
